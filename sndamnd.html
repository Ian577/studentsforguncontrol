The second ammendment: part of the Bill or Rights was ratified on December 15, 1791. It ensures the right of all Americans to bear arms; to posess weapons to protect themselves their rights and their property.

Because of the Second Ammendment it is difficult to regulate weapons. With many arguing that the second amendment makes the ban or regulation of weapons would be unconstitutional there is a challenge to find a legal way to regulate the sale of weapons.

There have been arguments that say regulating the sale of weapons through things such as more strict beackground cheks do not violate the second amendment because they do not prevent all Americans from accessing weapons, while still keeping them out of the hands of those who would use them for harm.

As an effort to end gun violence, in 2023 the Biden administration established the White House Office of Gun Violence Prevention. One of the offices main efforts was the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act which aims to lower gun violence by increasing the Federal age limit to buy a gun to 21, improving access to mental health, especialy for young adults, and granting new authorities to the Justice Department to prosecute firearm traffickers. The organization is still calling for congress to require safe storage of firearms, requiring stricter background checks for all gun sales, eliminating gun manufacturers immunity from liability, and enacting a ban on assault weapons with a high capacity.
